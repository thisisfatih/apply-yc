Founded PYP Media September 2010 together. Restructured as The Daily Muse July 2011.

## How far along?
22,000 beta users in September. First revenue deal with Uber for job posting and newsletter partnership launching October 12, 2011.

## Why this idea / domain expertise?
"We picked this idea because we are our customer: ambitious, confident women who don't want to find our next job on Monster.com..."

## What's new / substitutes?
"No one is currently tackling the 'smart content for women' space, and it's one we can begin to capture with front runner advantage."

## Competitors
LinkedIn, The Ladders, Marie Claire, LearnVest

## What do you understand that others don't?
"We understand the demographic, inside and out. Most of the people trying to hire these women, market to them, and entertain them are well outside of it."

## How will you make money?
Three streams: recruiting (newsletters, job boards, resume databases), training/professional development, advertising/branded content. Estimated potential: $40M/year.

## User acquisition
Syndication through Huffington Post, Forbes, Business Insider, Inc. Partnership with Refinery29 (500k subscribers).
