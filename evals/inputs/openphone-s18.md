# OpenPhone — YC S18 Application

## 50-character pitch
"Phone system meets CRM built for small businesses"

## What is your company going to make?
OpenPhone provides a phone system with integrated CRM capabilities for small business owners. A dedicated business phone number accessible through mobile, with personalized voicemail, call routing, shared team access, customer relationship management, texting and appointment scheduling.

## Location
Kitchener, Canada and San Francisco, USA

## Progress
Public launch January 2018. 1,500 users, 200% month-over-month growth, 32 paying customers after introducing paid tiers.

Revenue (monthly): $0 / $0 / $30 / $20 / $20 / $320

## Why this idea / domain expertise?
Founder worked at Joist.com serving trade contractors. Observed professional communication and organization correlated with higher earnings. Contractors relied on personal phone numbers for business, creating professional and organizational challenges. Co-founder Daryna scaled Vidyard GoVideo to 200k+ users.

## What's new / substitutes?
86% of small businesses use personal phone numbers for work. OpenPhone bridges phone systems and CRM into one unified interface. No existing product combines these for small business.

## Competitors
Traditional cloud phone systems (Google Voice, Sideline, Grasshopper) lack modern features. Modern alternatives like Dialpad target larger enterprises. Communication platforms (Front, Intercom, Drift) focus on email/chat, not phone. Social platforms fragment rather than unify.

## How will you make money?
Subscription: $10/month now, scaling to $50-200/month at maturity. 33 million small businesses in North America = $40B annually at $100/month average.

## User acquisition
Bottom-funnel: Google, AppStore, Play Store paid advertising. Referral programs, SEO, ASO for organic growth.

Seeking fast-paced program, experienced mentors. Cited YC alumni Front as evidence of similar products' scaling potential.
