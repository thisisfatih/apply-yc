# The Flex Company — YC S16 Application

## 50-character pitch
"We make FLEX, a more comfortable alternative to tampons"

## What is your company going to make?
FLEX is a disposable feminine hygiene product shaped like a diaphragm, designed for internal wear up to 12 hours and enabling mess-free period sex. The menstrual product market has remained largely unchanged since the 1930s, with 91% of women seeking alternatives. FLEX is not linked to toxic shock syndrome and is marketed as exceptionally comfortable.

## Most impressive thing (per founder)
"We have 30 years' experience using products we loathe." Collectively: consumer brand launches at major corporations, ecommerce growth experience (Erika, 10 years at JimmyJane), advisory support from medical and manufacturing specialists.

## Interesting project together
Organized a DJ event in San Francisco, brokering a deal with a nightclub that covered travel costs and product samples while generating signups and distribution opportunities.

## How far along?
April marks our 1-year anniversary and we've raised $225k. 200+ product trials, 20,000 trial signups with zero acquisition spend, 1,000 weekly signups growing organically. Product fully designed, manufacturer contracts drafted. Launch: April preorders, September customer shipments, 2017 retail expansion.

## Why this idea / domain expertise?
Three motivations: personal frustration with tampons and period stigma affecting intimate relationships; recognition of the "Year of the Period" movement with women demanding innovation; discovery of a neglected 20-year-old patented design allowing rapid development.

"We have 30 years' experience using products we loathe" and collective expertise spanning consumer brand launches, ecommerce growth, and advisory support from medical and manufacturing specialists.

## What's new / substitutes?
Direct-to-consumer distribution, millennial-focused branding, 12-hour wear without TSS risk, sexual functionality, environmental benefits. Existing options: tampons, pads (unchanged since 1930s), menstrual cups (DivaCup), subscription innovation startups (LOLA, Cora, THiNX).

## Competitors / who do you fear most?
Large consumer goods companies (P&G, Kimberly-Clark, Playtex, Energizer) represent primary competitive threats despite lacking millennial appeal. Secondary: Softcup (similar design), menstrual cups (DivaCup, Lunette), subscription/innovation startups (LOLA, Cora, THiNX).

## What do you understand that others don't?
"Women (and men) love our branding and positioning." Organic word-of-mouth and grassroots marketing resonates better than traditional retail channels. 25% of signups from men. Peer recommendations driving adoption.

## How will you make money?
Monthly subscription sales through ecommerce with free shipping. Revenue projections targeted $3M over two years with aspirations to reach $100M in five years.

## Surprising / amusing
"After using FLEX many women — including Erika and I — no longer get post-period yeast infections." Widespread unreported issues with traditional tampons and deep-rooted period stigma affecting women's product choices.
